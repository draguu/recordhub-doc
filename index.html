Installation

Step 1: Download

Download the record_hub.py file or ZIP archive from the GitHub repository.

Step 2: Install in Blender

  1. Open Blender and go to Edit ‚Üí Preferences ‚Üí Add-ons
  2. Click Install... button at the top
  3. Navigate to and select the downloaded file
  4. Enable the addon by checking the checkbox next to "3D View: Record Hub"

Step 3: Access the Panel

Find the addon panel in the 3D Viewport sidebar (press N key) under the "Timelapse" tab.

üí° Tip: For video export functionality, make sure FFmpeg is installed on your system or provide the path to the FFmpeg executable in the settings.

FFmpeg Installation

FFmpeg is required for video encoding. Download it from ffmpeg.org and either:

  • Add FFmpeg to your system PATH (recommended), or
  • Specify the full path in Settings ‚Üí FFmpeg Path

Overview

Record Hub is an automated viewport recording addon for Blender that captures smooth camera movements around your 3D scenes and exports them as timelapse videos.

‚ú® Key Features

  • Three Operating Modes: Orbit, Ping-Pong, and Follow Me
  • Smart Tracking: Follow selected objects or entire scenes
  • Automatic Recording: Continuous viewport capture at 60 FPS
  • Video Export: Create timelapse and/or realtime videos
  • Customizable Settings: Speed, smoothness, and quality controls

üéØ Operating Modes

Orbit Continuous Rotation

Smooth 360¬∞ rotation around the Z-axis (vertical). Perfect for showcasing 3D models and scenes from all angles.

  • Adjustable rotation speed (1-90¬∞/sec)
  • Optional selection tracking
  • Auto-zoom based on object size

Ping-Pong Back & Forth

Back-and-forth rotation within a defined range. Great for dramatic reveals and focused showcases.

  • Adjustable range (10-360¬∞)
  • Multiple easing options (Linear, Ease In/Out, Sine Wave)
  • Smooth direction transitions

Follow Me Viewport Following

Monitors other viewports and follows your camera movements after a delay. Perfect for recording your modeling process!

  • Configurable delay (0.5-10 seconds)
  • Smooth camera transitions
  • Multi-window support
‚ö†Ô∏è Note: Follow Me mode requires at least two 3D viewport windows open - one for your work and one for recording.

Quick Start

üé¨ Recording Your First Timelapse

1. Choose Your Mode

Select one of the three mode tabs at the top of the panel:

  • Orbit - For continuous 360¬∞ rotation
  • Ping-Pong - For back-and-forth motion
  • Follow Me - To record your viewport movements

2. Adjust Settings

Speed: Controls how fast the camera rotates (degrees per second)

Smoothness: Controls how smoothly the camera follows changes (0.01-1.0)

Track Selection (Orbit & Ping-Pong only)

Toggle this option to control what the camera focuses on:

  • Enabled: Camera follows selected vertices/faces/objects
  • Disabled: Camera orbits around entire object(s)

Auto Zoom: Automatically adjusts camera distance (10-300%)

3. Preview or Record

  • Click Preview to test your settings without recording
  • Click Record to start capturing frames
  • Press ESC to stop at any time
üí° Pro Tip: Always preview your settings before recording to ensure you get the perfect shot!

‚öôÔ∏è Settings & Export Options

Click the Settings button to access advanced options:

Output Directory

Choose where to save recorded frames and videos. Default: ~/Timelapse_Recordings

Export Formats

  • Timelapse: Sped-up version (24-120 FPS) - perfect for social media
  • Realtime: Full-speed recording (24-120 FPS) - preserves original timing

You can enable both formats to get two versions of your recording.

Video Quality Presets

  • Fast: Quick encoding, larger file size (CRF 28)
  • Medium: Balanced quality and size (CRF 23) - recommended
  • High Quality: Slower encoding, smaller file (CRF 18)

Auto-Cleanup Frame Files

Enable this to automatically delete frame images after video creation. Only enable after verifying your video exported correctly!

FFmpeg Configuration

Specify custom FFmpeg path or leave empty to use system PATH. Status indicator shows if FFmpeg is detected.

üìÅ Output Files

After recording, videos are saved with timestamped names:

  • timelapse_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS_timelapse.mp4
  • timelapse_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS_fullspeed.mp4

üé® Best Practices

  • Use slower speeds (2-5¬∞/sec) for smooth, professional results
  • Higher smoothness values (0.2-0.4) create more cinematic movement
  • Record shorter clips (30-60 seconds) for easier handling
  • Keep frame files until you verify the video quality
  • Use Medium quality for most use cases

Troubleshooting

üîß Common Issues

‚ùå FFmpeg Not Found Error

Problem: Video encoding fails with "FFmpeg not found" message.

Solution:

  • Install FFmpeg from ffmpeg.org
  • Add FFmpeg to your system PATH, or
  • Specify the full path in Settings ‚Üí FFmpeg Path
  • Verify installation by opening terminal/command prompt and typing: ffmpeg -version

üñºÔ∏è No Frames Captured

Problem: Recording stops with 0 frames captured.

Solution:

  • Ensure you have a 3D viewport visible and active
  • Check that you have write permissions to the output folder
  • Verify sufficient disk space is available
  • Try using the default output path first

üé• Choppy Video Output

Problem: Final video appears jerky or stutters.

Solution:

  • Increase the Smoothness value (0.2-0.4)
  • Lower the rotation speed for smoother motion
  • Ensure your system isn't overloaded during recording
  • Try using a higher FPS setting for export

üìÅ Cannot Find Recorded Files

Problem: Can't locate the output videos.

Solution:

  • Check the output path in Settings
  • Look for timestamped folders: recording_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS_frames
  • Check console for actual save location
  • If auto-cleanup is enabled, frames are deleted but video should remain

üîÑ Follow Me Mode Not Working

Problem: Camera doesn't follow other viewport.

Solution:

  • Ensure you have multiple 3D viewports open
  • Wait for the configured delay time (default 0.8 seconds)
  • Keep the view stable for the full delay period
  • The monitoring viewport must be different from recording viewport

‚ö° Performance Issues

Problem: Blender slows down or freezes during recording.

Solution:

  • Close unused windows and applications
  • Reduce viewport quality (use solid shading instead of rendered)
  • Use smaller viewport sizes
  • Record in shorter segments
  • Ensure you have sufficient RAM available
